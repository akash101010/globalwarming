Understanding Global Warming

What is Global Warming?
Global warming refers to the long-term increase in Earth's average surface temperature due to human activities, primarily the emission of greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), and nitrous oxide (N2O). These gases trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, leading to the greenhouse effect and resulting in a gradual rise in global temperatures.

Causes of Global Warming:

  • Burning Fossil Fuels: The combustion of fossil fuels releases CO2 into the atmosphere.
  • Deforestation: Trees absorb CO2, so deforestation reduces the planet's capacity to remove this greenhouse gas.
  • Industrial Activities: Manufacturing processes and agriculture contribute to methane and nitrous oxide emissions.
Impacts of Global Warming:
  • Rising Temperatures: Global average temperatures have increased, leading to heatwaves and droughts.
  • Melting Ice and Rising Sea Levels: Glaciers and polar ice caps are melting, causing sea levels to rise.
  • Extreme Weather Events: More intense storms, hurricanes, and wildfires occur.
  • Ecosystem Disruption: Changes in temperature disrupt ecosystems, affecting wildlife and agriculture.
  • Ocean Acidification: CO2 absorption by oceans leads to acidification, harming marine life.
Addressing Global Warming:
  • Reducing Emissions: Transitioning to renewable energy sources reduces CO2 emissions.
  • Energy Efficiency: Improving energy efficiency reduces overall energy demand.
  • Conservation and Reforestation: Protecting forests and planting trees helps absorb CO2.
  • Climate Policies: Implementing policies to limit greenhouse gas emissions is crucial.
  • Public Awareness and Action: Educating communities and promoting sustainable practices is essential.
